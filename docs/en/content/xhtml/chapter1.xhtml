1. What is Chemical Waste

Hazardous waste is defined as a substance that poses a hazard to human health or the environment when not properly managed. A chemical waste is considered hazardous if it exhibits one or more of the following characteristics:


Ignitable

generally liquids with a flash point below 60Â°Celsius.


Corrosive

generally aqueous wastes with a pH or 2 or less or 12.5 or greater.


Reactive

are wastes that unstable, explosive and capable of detonation or reacting violently with water.


Toxic

a chemical that poses a hazard to health or the environment.

Hazardous chemical waste includes:

  • Chemicals that can no longer be used for their intended use (aged or surplus inventory).
  • Mislabeled or unlabeled chemicals.
  • Abandoned chemicals.
  • Material in deteriorating or damaged containers.
  • Residuals in chemical containers.
  • Diluted solutions containing hazardous chemicals.
  • Used photographic fixer and developer.
  • Debris contaminated with a hazardous material (bio-waste, gels).
  • Chemicals from e-waste

Chemical waste can be both a liquid and a solid. It is important that we understand what constitutes chemical waste as you investigate these types of crimes. You should also be aware what constitutes chemical waste in your relevant legislation.
